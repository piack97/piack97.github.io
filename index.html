About Me

As the Matt Johns Instrumentation Fellow at Carnegie Observatories, I work on cutting-edge astronomical instrumentation projects that push the boundaries of our observational capabilities and enable science in the era of massive surveys. My research focuses on the optical design, construction, and commissioning of novel multi-object spectrographs in the optical to near-infrared wavelength regime.

Large-scale surveys (e.g. Vera Rubin Observatory, Roman Space Telescope, SPHEREx) require detailed spectroscopic follow-up that is more accessible from ground-based observatories now and into the future with the onset of extremely large telescopes (e.g. GMT and E-ELT). My goal is to lead a research group that designs, builds, commissions, and uses optical and infrared multi-object spectrographs to support the next generation of astronomical observatories.
